The streets are bloody Freedom is sacrificed

7 December 2019

Many students in Iran chanted anti-government slogans in several Iranian universities during the annual Student Day gathering. Pictures and videos posted on social media show students holding placards that read: "People's Tables Plundered in Bloody Month of Aban" in reference to the three-fold increase of gasoline price which led to mass protests throughout the country and left hundreds of protesters dead. In these videos students are seen chanting slogans about the killing of innocent people during November protests, students' unity with workers, people's resistance and against “neoliberalist” policies of the government.

Students have also been changing slogans against various regime officials including President Hassan Rouhani who has avoided the Student Day gatherings this year and Chief Justice Ebrahim Raeesi who will be addressing a separate rally by Basij militia students at Tehran University. Student rallies have been held on December 7 to commemorate a rally in 1953 during which three students protesting the official visit of the U.S. Vice President Richard Nixon were killed. (1)
